Read this way. Nova has built the bottom-up analytical spine of equity research: it ingests SEC filings (Step 1), runs a multi-method intrinsic valuation (Step 7), now sets an explicit 12-month price target (Step 8) and issues a 1–5 STARS recommendation (Step 9) — then stress-tests that call through an adversarial bear-versus-synthesizer debate (Step 10) that goes deeper than S&P's static chart even depicts. The remaining gaps are the human-relationship steps — calling and validating forecasts with management (Steps 2 and 4) — together with S&P Core-Earnings normalization (Step 6), peer relative valuation and sum-of-parts (Step 7), and scheduled ongoing re-coverage (Step 11). Under the hood, two engines split the work: Signals is a deterministic, reproducible quant layer, while Debate is an LLM research pod that argues from cited evidence.
